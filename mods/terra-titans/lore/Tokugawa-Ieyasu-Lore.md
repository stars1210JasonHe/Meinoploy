# Tokugawa Ieyasu — The Patient Shogun

> First shogun of the Tokugawa shogunate, Japan  
> Alignment: Patience / Stability / Reserves

## Background

Tokugawa Ieyasu ended a long era of civil war in Japan and founded a shogunate that brought more than two and a half centuries of peace and stability. Famed for his patience, he waited out rivals over decades before consolidating power.

His settlement emphasized careful management, controlled exchange with the outside world, and the steady accumulation of reserves — a long peace built on caution rather than conquest.

## Board Persona

On the board, Tokugawa absorbs losses. His deep reserves blunt the worst financial setbacks, and he acquires a little cheaper than most — the patient player who is hardest to knock down.

## Passive — Sakoku Reserves

Financial negative-event losses -20%, shielded by deep reserves. Property purchase price -10%.

## Style

Tokugawa endures by three principles:

- Reserves quietly built decide the long game
- A loss softened is a loss half avoided
- Patience outlasts boldness

He rarely wins the early sprint, but he is the last one standing when the storms pass.

## Relationships

- **Taejo of Joseon** — Two founders of enduring, long-ruling orders
- **Cyrus the Great** — Rulers who prized stability and durable administration
- **Mansa Musa** — Both understood that reserves are strength held in waiting

## Theme

> Wait, and keep your reserves —  
> the patient ruler outlasts the storm.
