# Genghis Khan — The Great Khan

> Founder and first Great Khan of the Mongol Empire  
> Alignment: Tribute / Mobility / Organization

## Background

Genghis Khan united the nomadic tribes of the steppe in the early thirteenth century and founded the largest contiguous land empire in history. Beyond his campaigns, he was an organizer: he established a written legal code, a relay communication network, and a meritocratic structure that promoted ability over birth.

His empire connected East and West, securing trade routes and the flow of goods, ideas, and tribute across Eurasia on a scale never seen before.

## Board Persona

On the board, Genghis collects tribute. He marks one holding as regulated and makes every rival who lands there pay a premium — control exercised through the roads and the toll.

## Passive — Steppe Tribute

Designate one owned property as regulated; opponents pay +20% rent there.

## Style

Genghis commands by three principles:

- Promote by ability, and the able will follow you
- Hold the route, and the route pays you
- A single well-placed toll outearns a dozen small claims

He turns one key holding into a tollgate that drains every traveler who passes.

## Relationships

- **Hammurabi** — Both impose a binding claim — one in stone, one on the road
- **Alexander the Great** — Restless commanders who measured success in distance covered
- **Moctezuma I** — Two rulers who built their power on systems of tribute

## Theme

> Hold the crossing,  
> and the world pays to pass.
