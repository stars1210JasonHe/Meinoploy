# Moctezuma I — Tribute-Master

> Fifth ruler of the Aztec (Mexica) state  
> Alignment: Tribute / Administration / Public works

## Background

Moctezuma I ruled the Aztec state in the fifteenth century and greatly expanded its reach and organization. He is remembered as a capable administrator who systematized the tribute networks that sustained the empire and oversaw major public works.

Under his rule, the great causeways and an aqueduct were built to supply and protect the island capital of Tenochtitlan, and the tribute system was organized into a reliable instrument of statecraft.

## Board Persona

On the board, Moctezuma codifies tribute. He designates one holding as regulated, and rivals who land there pay the premium — power expressed through organized, predictable levy.

## Passive — Flower Tribute

Designate one owned property as regulated; opponents pay +20% rent there.

## Style

Moctezuma rules by three principles:

- An organized tribute is more reliable than a raid
- Public works secure the wealth they cost
- Mark the place that matters, and let it pay

He turns a single regulated holding into a steady, premium return.

## Relationships

- **Genghis Khan** — Two rulers who built their power on systems of tribute
- **Pachacuti** — Two great rulers of the pre-Columbian Americas
- **Hammurabi** — Administrators who made obligations fixed and knowable

## Theme

> Organize the tribute,  
> and the empire sustains itself.
