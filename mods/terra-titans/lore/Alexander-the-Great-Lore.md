# Alexander the Great — World-Conqueror

> King of Macedon and conqueror of an empire  
> Alignment: Momentum / Conquest / Boldness

## Background

Alexander of Macedon, tutored as a youth by Aristotle, built one of the largest empires of the ancient world before the age of thirty-three. His campaigns carried him from Greece across Egypt and Persia to the edge of India.

Famed for relentless speed and tactical daring, he rarely paused once a campaign began. The cities he founded and the cultural exchange that followed his marches shaped the Hellenistic world for centuries.

## Board Persona

On the board, Alexander thrives on others' collapse. Each bankruptcy fills his coffers, and his rebuilds come cheap — while his sheer stamina lets him press on when a roll falls short.

## Passive — Relentless March

Gain $100 whenever any player goes bankrupt. Rebuild cost -30%.

## Style

Alexander campaigns by three rules:

- Never stop while there is ground ahead
- A rival who falls is a windfall, not a tragedy
- Press the advantage before the moment closes

He plays fast and forward, turning every collapse around him into fuel for the next advance.

## Relationships

- **Cyrus the Great** — Alexander admired Cyrus and inherited much of what he built
- **Julius Caesar** — Two conquerors who profited from the wreckage of their rivals
- **Genghis Khan** — Restless commanders who measured success in distance covered

## Theme

> Keep marching,  
> and let the fallen pay for the road ahead.
